Key Findings

1. Metadata Retention Is a UX Problem, Not a Deal-Breaker

YELLOW

Users are uncomfortable with metadata retention but would still use the product. The gap between comfort (2.79) and usage intent (3.40) means they want what the product does enough to tolerate retention they're uneasy about.

2.79 / 5.0 (comfort score, benefit frame)
Details
  • Benefit framing lifts comfort by +0.30 vs technical language
  • FP&A Directors are the most comfortable (3.06), CFOs the most skeptical (2.55)
  • Usage intent (3.40) is well above the midpoint despite low comfort
Recommendation

Don't lead with retention. Start with the free audit (full deletion), prove value, then ask for retention consent. Frame it as "your agent's memory," not "we keep your data."

2. Charge $49. Price Sensitivity Is Minimal.

YELLOW-GREEN

Dropping the price from $49 to $29 barely moves the needle. Demand is inelastic in this range. $49 captures 69% more revenue per user with negligible demand loss.

$49 scores 3.33 vs $29 at 3.42 — only 0.09 difference
Details
  • $49/mo purchase intent: 3.33
  • $29/mo purchase intent: 3.42
  • Full pricing page ($0/$49/$99): 3.32
  • "Would you replace an existing tool to pay?" scored 3.29–3.38 across all price points
Recommendation

Price at $49/mo. The budget trade-off scores confirm buyers see enough value to displace existing tools.

3. Leading with the Co-op Tanks Trust

RED

When the co-op is the lead message, trust drops by half a point compared to pure deletion messaging. This is a real, medium-sized effect. IT Ops shows the biggest drop (-0.69).

-0.51 points (co-op forward vs pure deletion)
Details
  • Pure deletion baseline: 3.13
  • Co-op forward: 2.62 (trust drops 0.51)
  • IT Ops drops from 3.16 to 2.47 — the security gatekeeper alarm fires
Recommendation

Never lead with the co-op in any customer-facing context. It triggers "this company wants my data" alarm bells.

4. The Privacy-First Hybrid Preserves Trust

GREEN

When the co-op is presented as a separate, optional add-on after the privacy promise, trust is statistically identical to pure deletion. The co-op doesn't hurt if it's clearly separated.

3.10 (only -0.03 vs pure deletion baseline of 3.13)
Details
  • Privacy hybrid: 3.10 vs pure deletion: 3.13
  • The delta (-0.03) is well within noise
  • Upload intent still dips slightly (-0.15), but trust holds
Recommendation

Always present the co-op as a separate, optional, revocable choice — never bundled with core product messaging.

5. Users Will Share Data for Benchmarks

GREEN

Both data sharing frames crossed the 3.5 threshold. Users find the benchmark exchange worthwhile regardless of whether they share vendor-specific or category-level data.

3.55 / 5.0 (category-level sharing)
Details
  • Category-level sharing: 3.55
  • Vendor-level sharing: 3.50
  • The 0.05 difference is not meaningful — both work
  • IT Ops scored 3.60 on category-level (their highest score in any study)
Recommendation

Launch with category-level aggregation as the default. Lower data sensitivity, same benchmark value. Offer vendor-level as a voluntary upgrade.

Persona Insights

Champion Buyer: FP&A Director
Highest scores across all 12 runs. Most comfortable with retention (3.06 vs CFO's 2.55). Highest purchase intent across all price points. Highest value exchange scores (3.60). CFO is the approval authority but not the champion.

Gatekeeper: IT Operations Manager
Security alarm fires hardest when co-op is forward-framed (2.47 vs 3.16 baseline). Highest category-level sharing score (3.60). Needs to see privacy-first messaging before trusting the co-op.

Approval Authority: VP Finance/CFO
Most skeptical persona (lowest scores in 11 of 12 runs). Shows largest messaging sequence effect (+0.41 for privacy-first). Needs FP&A Director champion to sell upward.

Go-to-Market Sequence

1
Free Tier (Full Deletion)
One-time contract audit with complete deletion in 60 seconds. Privacy-first messaging on landing page. Trust-builder. Target FP&A Directors in outreach.
2
Co-op Opt-In (After Audit)
After free audit, offer category-level benchmark sharing as separate, optional, revocable choice. Never bundle with core product. Frame as value exchange (benchmarks), not data requirement.
3
Paid Tier at $29/mo
AI agent with metadata retention (framed as "your agent's memory"). Earn retention consent after proving value. Launch at $29 for higher intent.
4
Price Increase to $49/mo
After generating real customer savings stories, increase to $49. Demand is inelastic in this range. Price sensitivity is minimal.

Overall Verdict

YELLOW LIGHT — Build the product with a gated co-op.

The co-op business model is conditionally viable. Users want what ShrinkMyStack offers. They will share data for benchmarks. They will pay. But they will not let you keep their contracts without feeling uneasy. The path forward: Free tier (trust-builder) → Co-op opt-in (after 30–60 days) → Paid tier ($29, earn retention consent) → Price increase ($49, after proof points).
