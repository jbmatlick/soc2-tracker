SaaS Revenue — The Foundation

$29/mo Pro tier targets the 5–200 employee company that has never heard of Zylo or Torii. No per-employee pricing, no sales call required, free permanent audit. 3× cheaper than Cledara Basic ($75/mo) — the current market floor.

Data Asset — The Moat

Every paid user contributes anonymous SaaS stack metadata (50–60% opt-in). At 2,500+ contributors, this becomes a licensable data product — renewal timing, stack composition, adoption trends — sold to intent data platforms and ABM vendors.

◉
The Flywheel

More users → better benchmarking data → higher opt-in rates → more DaaS revenue → fund more user acquisition. Erik Matlick built Bombora from 0 to 2,500 contributing sites in Year 1. He's doing it again with a SaaS product as the data collection layer.

The dual-revenue thesis in three sentences: ShrinkMyStack's $29/mo pricing creates a defensible SaaS business in a market white space that no incumbent is positioned to match (they'd have to cut prices 60–70%). Every paid user who opts in (projected 50–60%) generates proprietary SaaS stack metadata that becomes a licensable data product at Month 13 in the base case, commanding $25K–$100K annual contracts from intent data platforms, ABM vendors, and PE firms. The DaaS layer adds $1.7M–$5.3M ARR at 10,000 paid users — but only if we execute the privacy-first, incentive-driven opt-in model correctly, keeping the data play as the business model, never the brand promise.

Data Sharing Opt-In Design

Survey data mandates privacy-first messaging (Cohen's d=1.46 — one of the largest effect sizes in B2B research). Data sharing scores 3.5/5.0 (moderate positive); metadata retention scores below 3.0 (net negative). The co-op messaging framing incurs a −0.51 trust penalty. Design principle: data sharing is a background feature with an incentive layer, never the headline. Pro data sharers receive $5/mo credit + enhanced benchmarks; Team sharers unlock the Vendor Intelligence panel — aggregate renewal timing and negotiation leverage data.
