Section 2

Methodology

  • Metric: TTM Revenue ÷ Most Recent Headcount from 10-K filings
  • Universe: 53 companies — 19 DaaS, 19 SaaS, 15 AI-First
  • Data freshness: All revenue and headcount from latest available filings (Q3/Q4 2024 or Q1 FY2025)
  • Statistical tests: Kruskal-Wallis omnibus test; Mann-Whitney U pairwise with Bonferroni correction (α=0.05/3=0.0167); Spearman rank correlations
  • Exclusions: SMAR (taken private), EXPGY (non-USD reporting), CoreLogic (private), Refinitiv (part of LSEG)
  • Currency note: SAP revenue converted EUR→USD at 1.09 rate
  • Sources: SEC 10-K/10-Q filings via Perplexity Finance API. Employee counts from most recent annual filings.

Section 6

The Surprise: SaaS Beats DaaS

SaaS Median RPE
$491,227
DaaS Median RPE
$385,145
+27.5% SaaS Premium

SaaS companies generate more revenue per employee than DaaS companies, contradicting the common thesis that data companies are inherently more capital-efficient.

Why?

Higher Gross Margins

SaaS mean GM: 72.5% vs DaaS mean GM: 68.3%. Software distribution scales with near-zero marginal cost.

Scale Advantage

Mature SaaS companies (Shopify, Salesforce, ServiceNow) achieve massive revenue without proportional headcount growth.

Data Infrastructure Tax

DaaS companies require more headcount for data operations, curation, and compliance — labor that doesn’t scale like software.

Despite intuition that layoffs should “inflate” RPE by reducing headcount while revenue lags, the data shows no statistically meaningful difference. This may be because: (a) revenue also declined at many companies that cut, or (b) the layoff timing vs. filing date mismatch masks the effect.

Section 9

Classification Justifications

Three companies warranted extra scrutiny in cohort assignment.

FICO DaaS
Why DaaS, not SaaS?

FICO’s primary revenue derives from its proprietary FICO Score (used in >90% of US lending decisions) and analytics/data licensing. While it offers a SaaS platform (FICO Platform), its economic moat is the scoring data monopoly, not the software delivery model. Revenue is overwhelmingly driven by per-score licensing fees — a classic data-as-a-service model.

Counter-argument

FICO Platform is growing rapidly as a cloud-native SaaS offering. However, this still represents <30% of revenue.

CRWD AI-First
Why AI-First, not SaaS?

CrowdStrike’s Falcon platform uses AI/ML models as the primary threat detection mechanism. Unlike traditional signature-based cybersecurity (SaaS), CrowdStrike’s differentiation IS the AI — behavioral models that identify novel threats. The entire product thesis depends on AI superiority.

Counter-argument

CrowdStrike has SaaS-like subscription revenue and delivery model. However, the AI is not an add-on feature; it IS the product.

S AI-First
Why AI-First, not SaaS?

SentinelOne’s Singularity platform uses autonomous AI for endpoint detection and response. Like CrowdStrike, the AI models are the core product, not a feature layer on top of traditional software.

Counter-argument

Same subscription model as SaaS companies, but the core differentiation and product architecture is AI-native.

Methodology Notes
  • RPE Calculation: TTM Revenue / Most Recent Headcount. Revenue is trailing-twelve-month as of the latest quarterly filing. Headcount is from the most recent annual filing (10-K or 20-F).
  • Gross Margin: (Revenue - Cost of Revenue) / Revenue, from the same TTM period.
  • Market Cap: As of the data_date for each company.
  • Layoffs: Any publicly reported reduction-in-force within the 2024-2026 window.
  • Fiscal Year Variance: Companies have different fiscal year ends (e.g., Salesforce FY ends Jan 31, FactSet FY ends Aug 31). All revenue figures are TTM to normalize.
